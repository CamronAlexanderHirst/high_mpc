Introduction

This work presents a new method to combine policy search and model predictive control~(MPC). Our main contribution is a policy-search-for-MPC framework, in which the design of both state-independent and state-dependent high-level decision variables in the trajectory optimization is formulated as a policy search problem. The resulting framework allows for learning three kinds of high-level policies, including Gaussian policies, Gaussian linear policies, and neural network policies. We apply the proposed framework to address a challenging problem in agile drone flight, which is flying a quadrotor through fast-moving gates. Solving this problem is an important step towards deploying the vehicle in a highly dynamic environment. We show that the proposed approach successfully maneuvers a quadrotor to fly through a pendulum-like dynamic gate in the physical world. The key is having both a trained high-level policy for automatically selecting desired decision variables and an MPC for simultaneously trajectory planning and vehicle control. Our findings suggest that the proposed framework can achieve both adaptive and robust control performance of the physical robot.
